About This Project

Why?

The more words young children are exposed to, the better the developmental outcomes. Unfortunately this correlates with income, with some children having heard 30 million words fewer than their peers by age 3. Childrenâ€™s TV programming is a cheap and accessible way to bridge this language gap.

How?

As subtitles are transcripts of what children hear while watching TV, subtitle files (.srt and .xml) of the most popular children's TV shows on Netflix and Amazon Prime were downloaded and analyzed in Python

Generally, the first/oldest (available) season was used for analysis, with the second season added if seasons were fewer than 10 episodes

Who?

Hopefully, parents who are mindful of the content their children consume will find value in this analysis. Content providers looking for ways to analyze the shows they provide and are conscientious about the value they deliver
